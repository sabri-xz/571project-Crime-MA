Crime Dataset for the state of Massachusetts in 2019

This project is based on a dataset that contains all the details of the crimes that have been reported in the state of Massachussets in the year of 2019.

The dataset we have found originates from the FBI, which collects data on crimes that have taken place including but not limited to details of the crime, the offender, the victim, and any property that was a part of the crime.
The extensivness of this dataset can be used to show a lot of different trends that exist in the crimes that took place in that year, and may be usefull to many different people with different use cases, such as political, social, economical or more.

We considered many different users for our charts but finally decided on focusing more on the social aspect of the data targeted at anyone who may be interested in visualizing these trends, and not just government officals looking to make policy decisions.

The main focuses of our four visualizations below are:
1. Drug use among different age groups
2. Distribution of offenders and victims on the basis of age, sex, race and ethinicity
3. Trends on how the offender knew the victim on the basis of the type of crime
4. Number of crimes reported across the time of day, day of week and month of year


CHART 1 : Drug use among different age groups

The dataset that we used included details of the offenders including their age as well as the type of drug that was used.
We used these two variables to visualize the chart below

The first thing that we noted was that the age ranged from 13-78. The visualizations created of this huge range were extremely difficult to read and thus we deicded to consolidate the data and narrow down the age ranges into groups of 10, ranging from the age of 11 to the age of 80.
We also realized that it was equally important to see each age range and each drug as a seperate bar chart to truly understand the trends for these. A heatmap, while good to get an understanding as a whole, was not enough for someone who would want to understand trends of a praticular age group or a particular drug.

Thus we created a heat map of the entire consolidated data, along with corresponding bar charts that appear when the mouse hovers over any cell, to show all teh visualizations that one might need to make any observations with this data.
A tooltip also helps show the exact number of incidents with any one cell in the heat map, along with details on the Age group and the drug type for each cell.

CHART 2: Distribution of offenders and victims on the basis of age, sex, race and ethinicity

The dataset that we used had a lot of details of the offenders as well as the victims for every crime. This included data such as their age, sex, race and ethinicty, all of which is generally interesting to identify the trends in crimes with respect to who commits them and who they are commited against.

In order to identify these trends we decided to make pie charts to show the distribution of the offenders and victims across these 4 categories. We also decided to show the pie charts side by side so that it is easy to compare the distribution of the offenders with the distribution of victims.
A set of buttons on the top of the charts helps in toggling between the different categories, while a tooltip helps to understand the exact category, number of peeople, as well as the percentage in terms of all the offenders or victims

CHART 3: Number of crime incidents per victim/offender relationship

The dataset listed crimes by type as well as the relationship between the victim of that crime and the offender. This chart allows you to select a type of crime to view how many instances of this crime occurred for each victim/offender relationship, and automatically sorts the bars in descending order.

CHART 4

In the dataset, we were able to obtain information regarding the time and date for which incidents occured, and the types of incidents, with these information we created the visualisations below, the chart on the left displays the distribution of crimes on different hours of the day and different weekday/month (the x-axis can be changed with the button), and the chart on the right displays the distribution of incidents across different types of crime, by clicking on the slots on the chart on the left, the chart on the right will display the distribution of crimes from 2019, in Massachusetts, in the corresponding hour of the day and month/weekday, across different types.
